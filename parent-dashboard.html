ABOUT PTA

The Parent/Teacher Association of Castle Royal College, Lagos, was inaugurated on Saturday, 30th January, 2012 by the former headmaster Mr. Yusu K. Effah. The aim was to bring parents and teachers together for the welfare of the students and the school in general.

PTA PROJECTS

CLINIC

The P. T. A. converted a wooden structure in the school into a clinic at the cost of ₦ 120, 000 in March 2013 to cater for the welfare of students staff and their dependants.
Miss Ruqaya Ennin, a Nursing sister was temporarily in charge of the clinic, until in December, 2014 when a full time nurse was appointed and paid by the Government in the person of Miss Florence Mensah.
Each year the P. T. A. gives grant for the running of the clinic. The clinic was rehabilitated at the cost of ₦ 200,000 in 2013. Currently, the nurse in charge of the clinic is Miss Ernestina Mensah.

CORNMILL

The P. T. A. purchased a cornmill on 28th November 2013 at the cost of ₦195,600.00. In addition to the cost of the machine the P. T. A. constructed a structure to house the machine and purchased the necessary fittings for the installation of the machine at a total cost of ₦229,480.00. The total cost of the machine and its installation stood at ₦ 425,080 in 2013.
The aim for the purchase of the mill was to reduce the overhead cost of feeding students by milling all the corn-related food items in the school.

FURNITURE

In 2017 and 2018, the P. T. A. purchased 172 mono desks at a total cost of ₦894, 400.00 for the school.

INCENTIVE TO TEACHERS

In order to boost the morale of Teachers, the P. T. A. introduced in 2012, a Free lunch to Teachers. This incentive is still enjoyed by the Teaching as well as non-Teaching staff in the form of Noon snacks.
Furthermore, the P. T. A. introduced a Revolving fund in the form of a loan scheme to needy members of staff both teaching and non-teaching.

ACCOMMODATION

The P. T. A. supplemented with an amount of ₦748,000.00 for the rehabilitation of the old Dining Hall and converted part into a Living Quarters for some masters. The second phase of the rehabilitation was carried out in 1989 when the P. T. A. spent ₦1,200,000.00 to convert the other part of the building for residence of Two housemistresses.
